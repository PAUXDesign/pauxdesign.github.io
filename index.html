The UX audit

What I actually look for

Every audit covers the same critical areas, the ones that consistently cost startups signups, activations, and revenue.

Onboarding flow

Where do users drop off? What's time-to-value? Is the first session building habit or confusion?

Navigation & IA

Can users find what they need? Do labels match how users think?

Pricing page & conversion

Is your value prop clear? What friction is blocking the upgrade decision?

Visual hierarchy

Does layout guide attention to what matters? Is typography readable?

FAQ

Things founders often ask me

What's the difference between hiring you and hiring an agency?
With an agency, you pay for a team you'll never meet — account managers, project managers, junior designers doing the actual work. With me, you get one senior designer on every decision, at a fraction of the cost.
How long does a project take?
A UX audit is usually 1–2 weeks. A full product design project runs 4–10 weeks depending on scope. A Framer website takes 2–4 weeks. I work in stages, so you see progress quickly and never wait long between updates.
How does pricing work?
Fixed scope, fixed price — agreed before we start. A 50% deposit starts the project, balance on delivery. Book a call and I'll give you a price range within 24 hours.
What if I only need part of the process?
That's fine. If you already have wireframes and need UI design, we start there. If you have a design and need a build, I step in at that point. The process adapts to where you are.
What does the free call involve?
30 minutes. I look at your product or site live, tell you honestly what's working and what isn't, and give you at least three specific things to fix — whether we work together or not. No pitch, no pressure.
